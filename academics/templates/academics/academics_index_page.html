Pathways After Cambridge IGCSE

We provide a wide range of teaching support and resource materials for each Cambridge IGCSE subject.

A-Levels 

Many students choose to pursue A-Levels, a two-year program that provides in-depth study of chosen subjects. A-Levels are widely recognized by universities around the world and serve as a common pathway to higher education.

Foundation Programs

Some students may enroll in foundation programs offered by universities. These programs provide additional academic support and preparation for entry into degree programs.

Direct University Entry

In some cases, students may be eligible for direct entry into university programs based on their IGCSE results and other criteria set by the universities. This is more common in certain countries or for specific programs.

Advanced Placement (AP) Courses

In some regions, students may take Advanced Placement courses, which are similar to A-Levels. Successful completion of AP exams can earn college credit and contribute to university admission.

IB Diploma

Some students opt for the IB Diploma, an internationally recognized program that emphasizes a broad and balanced education. It is offered in many countries and is accepted by universities globally.

SAT/ACT Exams

Some universities, especially in the United States, may require or consider standardized test scores such as the SAT or ACT for admission. Students may choose to take these exams to enhance their university applications.
